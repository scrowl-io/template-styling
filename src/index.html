Bear Safety Essentials

Avoidance

  • Bears are NOT predictable.
  • Bears can kill even experienced outdoorsman.
  • The best way to avoid a bear attack is to avoid bears altogether.

Signs of Bears

Torn Up Dead Logs

They're searching for insects.

Claw Marks

Bears leave claw marks on trees they climb, also on saplings and signposts.

Hair on Trees

From rubbing against the rough bark to scratch themselves.

Day Beds

Shallow depressions of leaves, trampled vegetation, or shallow holes, often near feeding places.

Here's what you can do to avoid bears.

  1. Check with locals about recent bear activity.
  2. Travel in groups on established routes and stay close together.
  3. Don't be out early morning and in the evening when they typically feed.
  4. Make noise as you travel and work. They will know you are there and you won't surprise them.
  5. NEVER approach a bear. Stay at least 300 feet away.
  6. NEVER offer food to a bear.

Bear Tips

  • camera
    Use binoculars or a telephoto camera lens if you want to get a closer look at a bear from a distance.
  • air_freshener
    Avoid the scent of pets, perfumes, & cosmetics. Carry food or toiletries in bear resistant canisters.
  • cruelty_free
    Pay attention to other wildlife calling, it may signal that a bear is close and move in the other direction.
  • phishing
    If you fish, throw fish offal in fast-moving streams or deep lakes, not along the sides of streams or lakes.
  • forest
    Know where they are likely to feed, travel, and sleep so you can stay away.

Deterrents

Bear Spray

A type of pepper spray that can deter bears at close range. While the sprays are not foolproof, it is the best way of fending off a threatening or attacking bear, giving you enough time to escape.

Noise

Loud noises can scare a bear away, sometimes the sight & sound of bear spray will be enough.

Pyrotechnics

Make sure the kind you use is permitted where you are.

Bear Spray Caution

Wind, distance, rain, and expired shelf life can make bear spray useless.

To use it safely and effectively, read the directions carefully.

Surviving a Bear Encounter

  1. Stay calm and assess the situation.
  2. Talk calmly and firmly.
  3. Stand still so the bear knows you are not a prey animal.
  4. Do not turn your back.
  5. Do not run. If you can back away slowly.
  6. Leave the bear an escape route, it may attack when feeling cornered.

As we said earlier, most bears want to be left alone; they don't want to attack. They want to avoid you as much as you want to avoid them.

Surviving a Charging Bear

Bears charge at high speed on all four legs.

  1. First use bear spray.
  2. If the bear has made contact:
    • Play dead
    • Lie flat on your stomach
    • Cover your neck with your arms
    • Spread your legs to prevent being flipped over
    • Do not resist, do not move until the bear has left.
  3. Fighting back makes things worse but if a bear starts to eat you, target their eyes, nose, and face

Standing your ground may or may not work if a bear is charging you.

Sometimes a bear will feel threatened and leave.

But this will not work against a predatory grizzly bear attack and may not work against a black bear.
